# Engineering Philosophy

A short, honest list of the principles I actually work by. Not aspirations on a
poster, the calls I keep making when it's inconvenient.

I've spent about 25 years building software, mostly at small, independent,
product-led companies. These are the things that experience left me believing.


---

## What I believe about work and life

### Same role, same pay
People aren't commodities priced by their postcode. Pay should follow what you
bring, not where you live. Same role, same pay.

### Healthspan is a responsibility
Health isn't just personal upkeep, it's a responsibility to yourself, your
family, and the people you work with. A lifestyle that doesn't leave room for it
isn't sustainable, and it doesn't hold up over the long run.

---


## How I work with people

### A dance, not a fight
Work isn't war. I'll argue hard for the right call, then commit fully once it's
made, even when it wasn't mine. The goal is moving well together, not winning.

### People are 360 degrees
People are whole humans, not just their output. Everyone has high and low
moments, and how a team treats each other in the hard ones is who it really is.
Success belongs to the whole team.

### Clear and on the level
Be transparent, be clear. Most problems are miscommunication, not too little of
it. Ambiguity and byzantine process are where trust goes to die.

### Respect every craft
"Easy" is mostly what we call work we don't do ourselves. Every craft has its
details and quirks, so I assume the thing I don't understand is harder than it
looks, and I treat the people who do it that way.

### A team, not a family
A company is a team, not a family. I care about the people I work with, but it's
healthier to be honest about the relationship: a strong team with clear
expectations beats pretending we're family.

## How I work

### Bias to action
When in doubt, do. I lean toward shipping and learning over deliberating,
especially where friction is low and trying something is cheap.

### Reality beats the plan
Plans are guesses, and reality wins. They have their uses, but bending reality to
fit a plan is backwards. I keep judgment switched on, read the real situation,
and sleep on the big calls instead of following a rule because it's the rule.

### Calm beats frantic
Sustainable pace over grind. I've been through high and low tides, and steady
wins. Not everything is ASAP, and burning people out was never a plan.

### Joy of missing out
JOMO over FOMO. I don't chase every trend or ping. The deep, uninterrupted hours
are where the real work happens, and I'll miss the hype to keep them.

### Hold the line
A principle you keep 98% of the time isn't really a principle. The "just this
once" exceptions never stop. I'd rather hold a line completely, at work and in
life, than bargain it away one reasonable step at a time.

---

## What I build, and who I build it for

### Respect the user
Software should respect the people using it. No selling them out, no dark
patterns, no tricks. Earn the relationship honestly.

### An obligation to independence
I'm drawn to independent, profitable, customer-funded companies, the kind that
answer to the people using the product instead of investors or a board.

### Built to stay, not to flip
I'd rather build something made to last than something built to be sold.
Longevity, and living with the consequences of your decisions, beats the quick
exit.

### Stay and go deep
Staying with something pays off. Years in the same place build craftsmanship,
real understanding, and bonds you can't fake, and that depth raises the quality
of both the work and the life around it.
